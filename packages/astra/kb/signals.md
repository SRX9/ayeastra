A signal is the core unit of intelligence in AyeAstra: one meaningful change at a watched company, interpreted for your business. Signals are what fill your Feed, power your briefings, and feed Ask Astra's answers.

## Anatomy of a signal
Every signal carries: a finding (the fact — "PayBridge cut Pro pricing 20%"), why it matters (the interpretation — grounded in your positioning and priorities), a recommended action when warranted, a severity level, a confidence level, a category, and evidence links to the captured page snapshots that prove it. Many signals also note "what would change this assessment" — the honesty clause that tells you what evidence would raise or lower confidence.

## Where signals come from
The monitoring engine watches each entity's sources. When a page changes materially, the change is classified, matched against your business context, scored, and — if it clears your relevance bar — lands in your Feed as a signal. Signals are never invented: every one traces back to a real, timestamped page change you can inspect.

## Signal categories
Signals are categorized: pricing, packaging, launch, messaging, hiring, funding, partnership, regulatory, M&A, market entry, platform shift, narrative shift, reviews, and more. Categories drive routing (which alerts fire), module ownership, and filtering.

## Signal lifecycle
New signals arrive in the Feed. You can acknowledge, snooze (7 days), or dismiss them. Nothing is deleted — dismissed signals stay in the archive and remain searchable through Ask.
