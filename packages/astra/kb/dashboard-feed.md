The Feed (Dashboard) is your home screen — a live, prioritized stream of signals from every company you watch. Each card answers three things at a glance: what changed, why it matters to your business, and what to do about it.

## Reading a signal card
Every card shows a severity chip (critical / high / notable), the company name, the signal category (pricing, launch, hiring, funding…), and the date it was detected. Below that: the finding (what happened), why it matters (interpreted through your business context), and — when the engine is confident enough — a recommended action with a one-click "Track action" button. Evidence chips at the bottom link to the captured proof: the actual page snapshots the signal was derived from.

## Acting on signals
Each card has quick actions: Acknowledge (seen it), Snooze 7d (not now), and Dismiss (not relevant). You can also give feedback — Useful, Not useful, Wrong, or Knew it. Feedback isn't cosmetic: it feeds the learned scoring system, which adjusts how future signals from that company and category are prioritized for your org.

## Why the Feed stays quiet
The Feed only shows material changes. Cosmetic changes (styling tweaks, typo fixes) are archived but never surfaced, and everything that does appear was scored against your business context — your priorities, segments, and competitive posture. If the Feed looks empty, it means nothing that matters to you has changed, not that nothing is being watched.
