AyeAstra's honesty rule: no claim without evidence. Every signal, briefing section, insight, and Astra answer traces back to captured proof.

## What evidence is
When a watched page changes, the platform stores the before-and-after snapshots — HTML, extracted text, and a screenshot — with timestamps and content hashes. That capture is the evidence record. It's append-only: evidence is never edited or deleted, so an audit trail always exists.

## Citations
Signals show evidence chips linking to their proof. Briefings cite evidence per section. Astra cites signal and change ids inline in its answers. If you ever wonder "says who?", the answer is always one click away.

## Sharing evidence externally
Evidence records can be shared outside your workspace via public evidence links — each has its own share token and shows the captured proof without exposing anything else from your workspace. Useful for convincing a skeptical stakeholder with the actual screenshot.

## Why some answers refuse
Astra and the briefing engine refuse to synthesize when evidence is insufficient — "we haven't collected evidence on that" beats a confident guess. If you hit a refusal, the fix is usually to widen coverage (watch the entity, add the source) and let the archive fill in.
