A mission is a standing question you ask the engine to keep answering — "Win against PayBridge in mid-market", "Track pricing pressure in EU payments". Missions filter the whole platform through their lens.

## What a mission does
When you create a mission, you set a goal, pick the entities involved, and optionally define KPIs. The engine expands it into a watch spec — which categories to watch, what to look for, which leading indicators matter — that you can edit. From then on, relevant signals are pulled into the mission's brief automatically.

## Mission briefs
Each active mission keeps an auto-refreshed brief: the current state of play against your goal, built from evidenced signals with citations. Open a mission to read its latest brief, KPIs, and linked signals.

## Closing a mission
When a mission ends, close it to generate a retrospective — what happened, what the engine caught, what the outcome was. Retrospectives are institutional memory: they stay searchable so the next team member doesn't relearn the same lesson.

## Missions vs. watching
Watching an entity is passive coverage; a mission is an active goal. You can watch a company without any mission, but a mission focuses scoring and reporting on what you're trying to achieve.
